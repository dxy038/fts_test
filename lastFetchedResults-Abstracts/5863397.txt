There is a marked tendency to assimilate the scientific with the technical and to assume that anything that is organised in a structured manner is necessarily objective and neutral. But is everything the industrial world produces good and useful by nature? To deserve the adjective &#8220;technical&#8221;, writers should ask questions, even when provided with &#8220;facts&#8221; and &#8220;figures&#8221; that seem to be the essence of what they should write about. This may, in the end, help to achieve better products
