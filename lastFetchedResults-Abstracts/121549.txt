Objective: Repent of presence of hyalomma aegyptium tick from Testudo graeca turtle in Iran.
Design: Case study.
Animal: Two turtles.
Procedure: Clinical as well as laboratory diagnosis.
Results: In the spring 2001, 6 ticks (4 male, 2female) were observed and collected in 2 turtle, Testudo graeca in Kerman, province at south part of Iran. Our observation confirmed that they have been Hyalomma aegyptium, a common turtle parasite that was designated the type of subgenus Hyalommasta. This is the first report of H.aegyptium of mentioned Tortoise in Iran.
Concision: It is the first report of the presence of Hyalomtna aegyptium tick from Testudo gracca turtles in Iran.
