Nowadays, the administrative-financial corruption has become a worldwide problem and the governments are quite aware that corruption may bring about a lot of harms. This problem has undermined the rule of law, threatened the democratic organizations and finally distorted developing process of different economic sectors, especially the financial sector of life insurance. The main objective of this study is to evaluate the impact of administrative-financial corruption control on financial development of life insurance market in selected developing countries including Iran, during the years 1999-2011. For this purpose, firstly, the control of corruption index was used to measure the level of corruption in selected countries and then, using panel data technique, the model was estimated. The results of this study show that, variables of corruption control, development of the banking sector, risk aversion and life expectancy have positive and significant effects, and variable of inflation has significant and negative effect on financial development of life insurance market.
