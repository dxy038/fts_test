The main objective of this study is evaluating the reasons for families in Mashhad to shop or not to shop from municipality markets. Required statistics and data obtained by field survey, 260 completed questionnaires by the customers of municipal farmer's market and families who shop, shop less or don't shop at farmer's markets. The data was analyzed using discriminant analysis and considering 21 variables. The findings show that the most important effective variables in differentiating between families who shop, not shop or shop less at the farmer's market in order of importance are: the quality of fruit and vegetables, price, overall satisfaction from farmer's markets, existence and availability of parking, gender, geographical location, family access to markets and vendors behavior. Based on obtained findings, some recommendations were presented to increase the shopping of families from municipal markets
