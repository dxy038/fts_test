This article is a reflection of the rapid advancements in technology and its relationship with the engendering of social behavior. What can be done about the fact that technology is progressing rapidly and leaves little room for creating social norms and behaviors? More importantly, what is the role of school and university in this process? What role can educational technology play through its hidden curriculum, - one that plays an important role in the developing of Social norms? Finally, can one create incentives for acceptable behaviors through educational technology and its core objective of analytic thinking? This article is an attempt to reiterate the fact that humans must be creators of Technology and not created by it.
