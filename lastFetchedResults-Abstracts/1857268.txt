Noise pollution is one of the most important environmental problems of today's society and is considered a potential impact on human health. The main objective of this study was to evaluate the comparative assessment of noise pollution level zoning morning, noon and night at Birjand city. In this study, 43 sites were selected to evaluate and measure the noise level. The results showed that  in the morning period  the stations 7, 8, 15, 18, 20 to 37, 39 and 43 and in the afternoon period the stations 1,4, 6, 7, 11, 13, 15, 16, 18, 20, 21, 23- 39, 42 and 43 and also in the evening period the stations 2,4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 15, 16, 17, 18, 20, 30, 32 - 39, 42 and 43 have equivalent sound level more than 70 dB. Statistical analysis revealed that there is no significant relationship between the width of the streets and traffic volume and noise level. The results of the present study was that the traffic volume and noise levels mapping alignment for three different periods in the morning, noon and night were plotted using GIS software that Indicating a direct effect of the traffic volume at noise pollution level. The level of noise pollution of Birjand in different station were compared with standard level of noise in Iran which revealed that almost all of the residential stations have excessive amounts of standard and puts public health at risk, Therefore, the appropriate measures to reduce and control these pollutants is avoidable.
