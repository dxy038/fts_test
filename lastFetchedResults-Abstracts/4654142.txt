We designed In<sub>x</sub>Ga<sub>1-x</sub>As/InyAl<sub>1-y</sub>As quantum cascade laser (QCL) structures, based on the four-quantum well active region operating at lambda ~ 2.8-3.3 mum in terms of an objective function, i.e., z<sub>UL</sub> <sup>2</sup>(1 - tau<sub>L</sub>/tau<sub>UL</sub>)tau<sub>U</sub>, related to the optical gain, including dipole matrix element (z<sub>UL</sub>) and population inversion between electron transitions. For shorter wavelength emission, the higher conduction band discontinuity (DeltaE<sub>C</sub>) was achieved by changing the In mole fraction of In<sub>x</sub>Ga<sub>1-x</sub>As/InyAl<sub>1-y</sub>As strain-compensated layers. The use of strain-compensated In<sub>0</sub>.<sub>72</sub>Ga<sub>0</sub>.<sub>28</sub>As/In<sub>0</sub>.<sub>3</sub>Al<sub>0</sub>.<sub>7</sub>As pair (i.e, DeltaE<sub>C</sub> = 857 meV) leads to the shortest wavelength up to lambda ~ 2.84 mum with tau<sub>43</sub> = 3.96 ps, tau<sub>4</sub> = 1.21 ps, tau<sub>3</sub> = 0.55 ps and z<sub>43</sub> = 0.57 nm under an electric field of 94 kV/cm.
