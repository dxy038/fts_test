Explores the nature of strategic goals underlying composite information systems (CISs) and ways to increase the likelihood of success. The authors study a regional hospital and its relationships with its physicians as part of an actual case study for providing physicians and staff with a convenient interface to disparate hospital departments. Three approaches to motivate strategic alliances are identified: bidirectional benefits, cooperative payoffs, and asymmetrical control.
