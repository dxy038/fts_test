Background: The main objective of all organizations is to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the personnelâ€™s performance and this partly depends on effective management. Managers are valuable assets and studying their behavior and personality dimensions may provide important guide for effectiveness and efficiency improvement. In this connection the objective of this research is to determine the association between organizational effectiveness and position of control in managers of staff in Shiraz University of Medical Sciences.
Methods: The present research is a cross-sectional study. Statistical societies of this research include all personnel and managers of staff in Shiraz University of Medical Sciences. 168 subjects of 8 departments were selected through accidental-stratified sampling and the data for the managers of these departments were also collected. The data were collected using 2 questionnaires of organizational effectiveness and Ratters' position of control. Finally the collected data were analyzed using SPSS 15th software and Kruskal-Wallis and Mann-Whitney Tests. 
Results: The results of this research showed that there was a significant association between organizational effectiveness and its dimension with managers' position of control. Also we didn't find a statistical association between organizational effectiveness and managers' position of control according to their sex, managerial experience and education.
Discussion: As internal managers are more effective than external ones, we can appoint individuals with internal control position for management of organization. Also we can train managers to shift their external position of control to internal one.
