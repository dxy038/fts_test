<p><strong>Objective:</strong> The aim of our study is to determine the impact of cochlear implantation on anxiety anddepression in mothers of children with cochlear implant.<br /><strong>Materials and methods:</strong> In a Cohort design, 35 mothers of deaf children with cochlear implantion admitted to the clinic of Baqiyatallah hospital during January 2008 â€“ January 2010 were selected through systematic sampling. Information of demography, depression and anxiety are obtained from the parents by checklist and beck questionnaires, respectively.<br /><strong>Results:</strong> Mean depression and anxiety scores in cochlear implant candidates were more than cochlear implant recipients. The difference was significant in depression and anxiety (P=0.001).<br /><strong>Conclusion:</strong> It seems that cochlear implant use leads to decrease of depression and anxiety but still high prevalence of these complications than the normal population.</p>
