Abstract
The objective of this paper is to understand the relationship between sales skills and salesperson performance with the mediating role of Job Satisfaction. This was a survey and the aim was practical. The salespersons at active companies in manufacturing and distributing Hygienic & Cosmetics products constituted the population. A proportionate stratified random sampling applied. Data were gatheed through sample. 188 questionnaires received.  linear regression, Spearman correlation test, partial correlation test and Baron and Kenny’s methodology of mediator variable distinction were used for analyzing the data. The findings showed that the relationship between Sales Skills and Salesperson Performance among experienced Salesperson was positively direct. Also Job Satisfaction was showed to improve the influence of Sales Skills on Salesperson Performance.

Keywords: Sales Skills; Job Satisfaction; Salesperson Performance; Baron and Kenny’s methodology.
